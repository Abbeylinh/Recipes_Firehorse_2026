RECIPE 00  ·  MAKE 2 DAYS AHEAD

Pimento Cheese

House-made sharp cheddar and cream cheese spread with diced pimentos, smoked paprika, and a whisper of hot sauce

MAKES 2.5 LBS  ·  SERVES 4 BOARDS FOR 60 GUESTS  ·  MAKE 2 DAYS AHEAD

Ingredients

  • 2.5 lbs sharp cheddar — shredded fresh, never pre-shredded
  • 8 oz cream cheese — block form, softened to room temperature
  • 1½ cups mayonnaise
  • 2 x 4oz jars diced pimentos — drained · reserve 1 jar for garnish
  • 1 tbsp hot sauce
  • 3 tsp apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika — plus more for dusting
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • Salt to taste

Board Components — Per Board

  • Butter crackers — Ritz or Club · 2–3 sleeves per board
  • Water crackers · 1 sleeve per board
  • Texas toast — lightly toasted, cut into triangles
  • Celery sticks — refreshed in ice water before service
  • Bread & butter pickles — small pile
  • Pickled okra — standing upright on board
  • Hot honey — small drizzle alongside

Method

Shred the cheese

Shred cheddar fresh using a box grater or food processor. Do not use pre-shredded — it has an anti-caking coating that prevents smooth blending and ruins the texture.

Soften cream cheese

Let cream cheese sit at room temperature for at least 30 minutes before mixing. Cold cream cheese will leave lumps that never fully incorporate.

Mix the base

In a large bowl combine softened cream cheese and mayonnaise. Beat with a hand mixer or wooden spoon until smooth and fully combined. No lumps.

Add the cheddar

Add shredded cheddar in two batches, folding in with a spatula between additions. The mixture will be thick and slightly chunky — that is correct. Do not over-mix into a paste.

Season and add pimentos

Add drained pimentos from 2 jars, hot sauce, ACV, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne, and black pepper. Fold gently. Taste — should be rich, tangy, slightly spicy. Adjust seasoning. Add salt if needed.

Refrigerate overnight

Transfer to airtight containers and refrigerate. Pimento cheese made 2 days ahead is significantly better than same-day. The flavors meld and the texture firms up to the ideal spreadable consistency.

Assemble boards day of

Pull from fridge 30 minutes before service. Scoop into a crock centered on each slate board. Dust top with smoked paprika. Scatter extra diced pimentos on top. Arrange crackers, celery, pickles, toast points, and pickled okra around the crock. Drizzle hot honey alongside. Beer cheese crock and pretzel bites go on the same board.

Kitchen Notes

Make 2 days ahead — non-negotiable. Same-day pimento cheese is flat and one-dimensional. Two-day is a completely different and better product.

Board tip: the crock should be centered and generous. Surround it abundantly — a sparse board looks cheap. Restock every 45–60 minutes.

Pickled okra doubles as a gin rickey garnish at the bar — reserve 1 jar specifically for the bartender.

RECIPE 01  ·  MAKE THE DAY BEFORE

Bourbon Baked Beans

Navy beans slow-cooked with bourbon, bacon, molasses, and apple cider vinegar

SERVES ~60 GUESTS  ·  CHAFING DISH  ·  MAKE THE DAY BEFORE

Ingredients

  • 4 x 28oz cans navy or Great Northern beans — drained and rinsed
  • 2.5 lbs regular cut bacon — diced into ½ inch pieces
  • ½ cup bourbon — EARLY (added to bacon)
  • ½ cup bourbon — TO FINISH (last 30–45 minutes)

Sauce Base

  • ½ cup molasses
  • ½ cup brown sugar — easy on the sugar
  • 1 cup ketchup
  • ½ cup apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tbsp stone ground mustard
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tbsp hot sauce

Spices

  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp cayenne
  • 1 tsp black pepper

Liquid

  • 2 cups water or chicken stock

Method

Render the bacon

In a large heavy pot over medium heat, cook diced bacon until fat is rendered and bacon is lightly crispy. Do NOT drain — the bacon fat stays in the pot. It is the flavor base.

Build the sauce

Add ½ cup bourbon to the bacon and fat. Let sizzle and reduce 1 minute. Add ketchup, molasses, brown sugar, ACV, stone ground mustard, Worcestershire, hot sauce, paprika, cayenne, and black pepper. Stir to combine and bring to a gentle simmer.

Add beans and liquid

Add all drained rinsed beans. Add 2 cups water or stock — enough to make beans loose and saucy. They will thicken as they cook. Stir everything together well.

Slow cook

Transfer to slow cooker on LOW for 8 hours OR cover and bake at 300°F for 3–4 hours. Stir occasionally. If too thick add a splash of water or stock.

Finish — critical step

In the last 30–45 minutes, stir in ¼–½ cup bourbon. Taste to adjust. This finishing addition brings the bourbon forward without cooking it away.

Taste and adjust

Beans should eat: savory, tangy, lightly sweet — not heavy. Adjust with more ACV for tang, more brown sugar for sweetness, or hot sauce for heat.

Hold and serve

Transfer to chafing dish on LOW. Holds beautifully for 3–4 hours. Stir every 30–40 mins. If they tighten add a small splash of water and stir through.

Kitchen Notes

Make the day before — beans improve significantly overnight. Reheat gently in chafing dish morning of event.

Use REGULAR cut bacon NOT thick cut — it renders fat more completely and integrates better into the sauce.

The two-stage bourbon addition gives depth without alcohol sharpness. Taste before the second addition.

RECIPE 02  ·  MAKE 1 DAY AHEAD  ·  MAKE 2 BATCHES SEPARATELY  ·  DO NOT DOUBLE

Kentucky Beer Cheese

Warm sharp cheddar and cream cheese blended with craft beer, stone ground mustard, garlic, and cayenne · a Louisville tavern classic

MAKES ~2 LBS PER BATCH  ·  MAKE 2 BATCHES — DO NOT DOUBLE  ·  SERVED WARM IN 10OZ CROCKS

Ingredients — Per Batch

  • 8 oz cream cheese — block form, softened to room temperature
  • 1 lb sharp cheddar — shredded fresh
  • 12–16oz craft beer — amber ale or lager · nothing too hoppy
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp stone ground mustard
  • 1 tbsp dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp garlic — minced fresh
  • ½ tsp cayenne pepper
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp kosher salt — more if needed

For Serving

  • 4–5 lbs soft pretzel bites — frozen reheat is perfectly fine

Method

Make the roux

In a medium saucepan over medium heat melt butter. Add flour and whisk together for 1–2 minutes until the raw flour smell cooks out and the mixture turns a light golden color.

Add the beer

Slowly pour in beer while whisking constantly. The mixture will bubble up — keep whisking until smooth. Bring to a gentle simmer. Add minced garlic and cook 1 minute.

Add cream cheese

Add softened cream cheese in chunks, whisking after each addition until fully melted and smooth.

Add cheddar

Reduce heat to LOW. Add shredded cheddar in three batches, whisking after each until fully melted before adding the next. Never rush this on high heat — cheddar added too fast will break and turn grainy.

Season

Add stone ground mustard, dijon, cayenne, smoked paprika, black pepper, and Worcestershire. Stir to combine. Taste — should be sharp, slightly bitter from the beer, warm from the cayenne, deeply cheesy.

Adjust consistency

If too thick add a splash of beer and stir through. Should coat a spoon but be loose enough to scoop easily. It tightens as it cools — err on the looser side.

Refrigerate overnight

Transfer to airtight container and refrigerate overnight. It improves significantly as it rests.

Reheat day of

Reheat gently in a small saucepan over LOW heat, stirring constantly. Add a splash of beer or water if it has tightened up. Transfer to a 10oz ceramic crock. Keep warm over a very small flame or chafing dish insert on LOW throughout service.

Serve

Place warm 10oz beer cheese crock alongside the pimento cheese crock on each board. Arrange soft pretzel bites alongside. Refill from a warm reserve pot in the kitchen as needed.

Pretzel bites

Reheat frozen pretzel bites in a 375°F oven for 8–10 minutes until warm and slightly crispy. Do not microwave. Reheat in batches and keep warm covered with foil.

Kitchen Notes

Make 2 batches — do NOT double the recipe. Make each batch separately for best results.

Beer choice matters — use amber ale or lager. Avoid IPAs or heavily hopped beers — the bitterness amplifies as it cooks.

If the sauce breaks (grainy or separated): remove from heat immediately, add a splash of cold beer, and whisk vigorously. Keep heat on LOW and never rush adding the cheese.

RECIPE 03  ·  MAKE THE DAY BEFORE

Quick Pickles

Bright, tangy, and crisp — made the day before, best after a few hours

SERVES 12–15 BOLOGNA SANDWICHES  ·  CONDIMENT  ·  MAKE THE DAY BEFORE

Ingredients

  • 4 medium cucumbers — sliced thin, 1/8 inch rounds
  • 1 small white onion — sliced paper thin
  • 1.5 cups apple cider vinegar
  • ½ cup water
  • 5 tbsp sugar
  • 3 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper — whole or cracked
  • 2 tbsp mustard seeds
  • ½ tsp celery seed
  • ½ tsp red pepper flakes

Method

Make the brine

Combine ACV, water, sugar, salt, black pepper, celery seed, and red pepper flakes in a small saucepan. Bring to a boil, stirring until sugar and salt fully dissolve. Remove from heat and cool 10 minutes.

Prep the vegetables

Slice cucumbers into thin rounds — about 1/8 inch. Slice onion paper thin. Layer cucumbers and onion together in a clean jar or container.

Pack and brine

Pour warm brine over cucumbers and onion. Make sure everything is submerged. Weigh down with a small plate or zip lock bag filled with water if needed.

Cool and refrigerate

Let cool to room temperature uncovered — about 30 minutes. Cover and refrigerate overnight. Good after 2 hours but significantly better the next day.

Serve

Drain before serving. Arrange in a small ramekin or pile directly on the platter next to the fried bologna. Label with a small chalkboard card.

Kitchen Notes

Make the day before — pickles improve overnight. These are a condiment not a featured item.

Mustard seeds add a pleasant pop of texture and mild heat — do not skip them.

The brine can be reused for a second batch if needed.

RECIPE 05  ·  MAKE THE DAY BEFORE  ·  MAKE BEFORE DEVILED EGGS

Candied Bacon Twists

Thick-cut bacon lacquered with brown sugar and cayenne — baked until glossy, served upright in mason jars

MAKES ~80–90 TWISTS  ·  ROOM TEMPERATURE STORAGE ONLY  ·  CRUMBLE SOME FOR DEVILED EGG VARIATION 1

Ingredients

  • 6.5 lbs thick-cut bacon
  • 2 cups brown sugar
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • 1.5 tbsp cayenne pepper
  • 2 tbsp cracked black pepper
  • 3 tsp smoked paprika
  • ¼ cup bourbon — optional but recommended

Method

Preheat and prep

Preheat oven to 375°F. Line two large sheet pans with foil and place WIRE RACKS on top. The wire rack is non-negotiable — it lifts bacon off the pan so the glaze sets rather than pooling in fat.

Make the glaze

Combine brown sugar, salt, cayenne, black pepper, and smoked paprika in a bowl and mix well. If using bourbon stir it in. Mixture will be a loose damp sugar.

Coat the bacon

Working one strip at a time, press each strip firmly into the sugar mixture coating both sides generously. Do not be shy — the sugar forms the lacquer. Shake off excess that will not adhere.

Twist and place

Hold each coated strip at both ends and twist gently 2–3 times to form a loose spiral. Lay on wire rack with space between each. Do NOT overlap.

Bake

Bake at 375°F for 20–25 minutes until deeply caramelized and glossy. Watch carefully after 18 minutes — sugar goes from perfect to burned quickly. Twists should look mahogany colored and glossy, not pale.

Cool completely

Remove from oven and let cool on rack at least 15 minutes before touching — sugar is molten hot and will burn. As it cools it hardens into a glassy candy coating. Do NOT stack while warm — they will stick together permanently.

Reserve crumble for deviled eggs

Once completely cool, crumble a small portion of the twists into rough pieces. Store separately in an airtight container at room temperature. This is the garnish for Deviled Egg Variation 1 (Recipe 04).

Store and serve

Store remaining twists in airtight container at ROOM TEMPERATURE. DO NOT REFRIGERATE — sugar absorbs moisture and goes sticky. Day of event stand twists upright in wide-mouth quart mason jars.

Kitchen Notes

Make BEFORE the deviled eggs — Variation 1 requires candied bacon crumble as a garnish (Recipe 04).

Keep at room temperature in airtight container — NEVER refrigerate. Sugar absorbs moisture and the twists go limp.

Wire rack is essential — do not skip it. Work in oven batches if needed.

RECIPE 04  ·  FILLING DAY BEFORE  ·  GARNISH MORNING OF EVENT  ·  REQUIRES CANDIED BACON FROM RECIPE 05

Deviled Eggs Three Ways

Classic Southern deviled eggs — made the day before, garnished morning of event

180 HALVES FROM 90 WHOLE EGGS  ·  THREE VARIATIONS  ·  60 HALVES PER VARIATION

Ingredients

  • 90 large eggs (buy 8 dozen — allows for cracking losses)
  • 2 cups mayonnaise
  • ½ cup sour cream
  • 4 tbsp dijon mustard
  • 4 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tsp hot sauce
  • 3 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper

Variation 1 Topping — 60 halves

  • Candied bacon crumble (from Recipe 05) + smoked paprika dusting

Variation 2 Topping — 60 halves

  • Pickled jalapeño slice + fresh chive

Variation 3 Topping — 60 halves

  • Whole grain mustard dot + fresh dill sprig

Method

Hard boil the eggs

Place eggs in large pot, cover with cold water by 1 inch. Bring to boil. Once boiling TURN OFF heat, cover, let sit exactly 12 minutes. Transfer immediately to ice bath for 15 minutes. Peel under cold running water.

Halve and separate

Slice each egg in half lengthwise. Pop yolks into large mixing bowl. Arrange whites on paper towel-lined sheet pans — must be completely dry or filling slides. Refrigerate whites while making filling.

Make the filling

Mash all yolks with fork until no lumps remain. Add mayonnaise, sour cream, dijon, ACV, hot sauce, salt, and black pepper. Mix until completely smooth. Taste — should be rich, tangy, well seasoned. Pipeable but not runny.

Pipe and refrigerate

Transfer filling to piping bag or zip lock bag with corner snipped. Pipe a generous rosette into each white half. Cover sheet pans tightly with plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight.

Garnish morning of event

Variation 1: candied bacon crumble + smoked paprika dusting. Variation 2: pickled jalapeño slice + fresh chive. Variation 3: dot of whole grain mustard + small dill sprig.

Plate and label

Arrange on large round platters in three distinct sections — one variation per section. Label each with a small chalkboard card. Refrigerate until 20 minutes before service then bring to room temperature.

Kitchen Notes

Make Recipe 05 (Candied Bacon Twists) BEFORE garnishing eggs — Variation 1 requires candied bacon crumble.

Make filling the day before. Add garnishes MORNING OF EVENT ONLY — not the night before or they go soggy.

Keep refrigerated until 20 minutes before service. Three distinct labeled sections on the platter so guests identify each variation.

RECIPE 06  ·  MAKE THE DAY BEFORE

Lexington Piedmont Slaw

Finely chopped cabbage with ketchup and ACV — a condiment for the pulled pork slider, not a standalone side

SERVES 90 PULLED PORK SLIDERS AS A TOPPING  ·  SMALL BOWL ALONGSIDE CHAFING DISH  ·  MAKE THE DAY BEFORE

Ingredients

  • 1 medium head green cabbage — finely chopped
  • ½ small head red cabbage — finely chopped (optional — for color)
  • 3–4 medium carrots — grated (optional)
  • ¾ cup ketchup
  • ⅔ cup apple cider vinegar
  • 3 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 1 tsp hot sauce
  • 2 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp celery seed

Method

Chop the cabbage

Chop green and red cabbage very finely — this is NOT a shredded slaw. Use food processor pulsed 3–4 times per batch, box grater on coarse side, or sharp knife. Pieces should be roughly the size of a corn kernel. Do not over-process into mush.

Salt and drain

Toss chopped cabbage with 1 tsp salt in a large colander. Let sit 20 minutes — salt draws out excess water so the dressing does not get watery. After 20 minutes squeeze cabbage firmly with your hands to remove as much liquid as possible.

Make the dressing

Whisk together ketchup, ACV, sugar, red pepper flakes, hot sauce, black pepper, and celery seed until fully combined. Taste — should be tangy, slightly sweet, with background heat.

Dress and rest

Pour dressing over drained cabbage and toss thoroughly. The slaw will look lightly pink from the ketchup — this is correct and characteristic of Lexington style. Cover and refrigerate at least 2 hours. Overnight is better.

Serve

Taste before service. Should be tangy forward with mild heat — not sweet and not creamy. Serve in a small to medium bowl on the slider station alongside pulled pork chafing dish. Label: Lexington Piedmont Slaw.

Kitchen Notes

This slaw is a CONDIMENT not a side dish — portion the bowl accordingly. A medium bowl is right.

The salting and draining step is critical — if cabbage is not properly drained the dressing gets watery within an hour on the table.

Make the day before. Overnight refrigeration is significantly better than same-day.

RECIPE 07  ·  HYBRID METHOD  ·  START SMOKER 8–9 AM THURSDAY  ·  PULL FRIDAY  ·  REHEAT SATURDAY

Smoked Tangy Bourbon Pulled Pork

Hybrid method — pork shoulder slow-smoked for bark and flavor, then finished with a tangy bourbon braise

SERVES ~90 SLIDERS (60–70 GUESTS)  ·  CHAFING DISH  ·  BUILD-YOUR-OWN SLIDER STATION WITH MARTIN’S ROLLS & PIEDMONT SLAW

Ingredients

  • 28–30 lbs pork shoulder / Boston butt — bone-in preferred — 2–3 shoulders
  • 96 Martin’s potato slider rolls for serving

Dry Rub

  • 10 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 6 tbsp kosher salt
  • 6 tbsp black pepper
  • 3 tbsp cayenne pepper
  • 6 tbsp garlic powder
  • 6 tbsp onion powder
  • ¾ cup brown sugar — applied separately after rub

Braising & Finishing Liquid

  • 2 cups apple cider vinegar
  • 1 cup bourbon
  • 1½ cups water or chicken stock
  • ¾ cup hot sauce
  • 3 tbsp stone ground mustard
  • ¼ cup apple cider vinegar — FINISHING SPLASH ONLY — do not cook in

Method

WEDNESDAY — PREP

Make the dry rub

Combine smoked paprika, kosher salt, black pepper, cayenne, garlic powder, and onion powder in a bowl. Mix well. Set brown sugar aside — applied separately after the rub.

Rub the pork

Pat pork shoulders completely dry. Score fat cap in crosshatch pattern 1 inch apart, 1/4 inch deep. Rub dry rub aggressively over every surface. Pat brown sugar evenly over the rub. Wrap tightly and refrigerate overnight — minimum 4 hours.

THURSDAY — SMOKE PHASE (Flavor + Bark)

Preheat smoker

Set smoker to 225–250°F. Use applewood, hickory, or a blend of both. Allow 30–45 minutes to come to temperature and stabilize before loading meat. Start by 8–9 AM.

Check grate space

Confirm all shoulders fit with airflow around each piece. If Seth’s smoker cannot fit all at once, stagger them — start the first batch early and add the second once space opens. Do not stack or crowd.

Smoke the pork — unwrapped

Place pork directly on grates fat side up. Smoke 6–8 hours until: (1) deep reddish-brown bark forms, (2) internal temp reaches 160–170°F, (3) surface is set and not wet or soft.

⚠ DO NOT ADD LIQUID DURING SMOKE PHASE

Moisture prevents bark from forming. This phase is about building the crust. No liquid until the wrap.

THURSDAY — WRAP & FINISH (Tender Phase)

Transfer and add liquid

Move pork into deep foil pans or wrap tightly in heavy-duty foil. Add approximately 75% of the braising liquid around (not over) the pork. Reserve remaining 25% for finishing.

Continue cooking

Return to smoker or oven at 250–275°F. Cook 3–5 additional hours until: (1) internal temp reaches 200–205°F, (2) a probe slides in with no resistance.

FRIDAY — PULL, FINISH & STORE

Rest the pork

Remove from heat and rest uncovered 20–30 minutes. Do not skip — resting allows juices to redistribute.

Reserve all liquid

Pour all cooking liquid from the foil pans into a container. DO NOT DISCARD. Skim excess fat from the surface if desired. This is your finishing sauce.

Pull the pork

Pull into long strands using two forks or gloved hands. Discard bone and large fat pieces. Meat should pull effortlessly — if any section resists, return to oven covered at 275°F for another 30–45 minutes.

Finish and sauce

Return pulled pork to a large pot or hotel pan. Add reserved liquid gradually — start with half. Pork should be moist but not soupy — glossy not wet. Add finishing ACV splash and stir. Taste and adjust — more ACV for tang, more hot sauce for heat.

Cool and store — critical

Spread pork in shallow pans. Refrigerate uncovered until cool, then cover. Store refrigerated overnight. Pork held overnight improves significantly — flavors meld and texture becomes even more tender.

SATURDAY — REHEAT & SERVICE

Reheat

Transfer to chafing dish or hotel pans. Reheat gently: oven at 275°F covered OR chafing dish with lid. Add a small splash of water or reserved liquid if pork has tightened overnight.

Hold and serve

Hold on LOW heat. Stir every 30–40 minutes. If pork tightens add a small liquid splash and stir. Stack Martin’s rolls alongside. Set Piedmont slaw bowl next to chafing dish. Guests build their own sliders.

Kitchen Notes

Hybrid method: smoke for flavor and bark, braise for moisture control and tenderness. Best of both methods.

Start smoker by 8–9 AM Thursday. Full timeline: smoke 6–8 hrs + wrap and finish 3–5 hrs = 9–13 hrs total cook time.

Confirm smoker grate space before Thursday. 2–3 shoulders need airflow around each piece. Stagger if needed — do not stack or crowd.

30 lbs raw at ~60% yield = ~17–18 lbs cooked = ~90 sliders at 3oz each.

Do not oversauce — pork should be glossy, not wet. Add liquid gradually and taste as you go.

Pork held overnight improves significantly. Friday pull + Saturday reheat is the ideal sequence.

If any section of pork resists pulling, return to oven at 275°F covered for another 30–45 minutes.

RECIPE 08  ·  REMOULADE DAY BEFORE  ·  SHRIMP DAY OF  ·  TWO WAVES

Cajun Shrimp with House Remoulade

Gulf shrimp pan-seared in cajun brown butter — wide shallow bowl at room temperature — two batches

SERVES 60 GUESTS  ·  ORDER 21–25 COUNT SPECIFICALLY  ·  WAVE 1 AT 1:30 PM (4 LBS)  ·  WAVE 2 AT 2:30 PM (4 LBS)

Ingredients

  • 8 lbs shrimp — ORDER 21–25 COUNT — peeled and deveined
  • 10 tbsp cajun seasoning
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 3 tsp cayenne
  • 3 tsp black pepper
  • 3 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 10 tbsp butter
  • 4 garlic cloves — minced
  • Zest of 1 whole lemon
  • Zest of 1 whole lime
  • 2 lemons — juice of, plus wedges for serving
  • 1 bunch flat-leaf parsley — roughly chopped

House Remoulade — Make Day Before

  • 1 cup mayonnaise
  • 3 tbsp dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp hot sauce
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 garlic clove — minced
  • 3 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 tsp cajun seasoning

Method

Make remoulade — day before

Whisk together all remoulade ingredients until smooth. Taste — should be tangy, slightly spicy, and rich. Cover and refrigerate overnight. It improves significantly as it sits.

Season the shrimp

Combine cajun seasoning, smoked paprika, cayenne, black pepper, salt, and brown sugar. Toss shrimp until well coated. Do this RIGHT BEFORE cooking — not hours ahead or salt draws out moisture.

Make cajun brown butter

Heat large heavy skillet or cast iron over HIGH heat until very hot. Add butter and let foam and begin to brown slightly. Add minced garlic and cook 30 seconds until fragrant. Brown butter — not burned butter.

Sear the shrimp

Add seasoned shrimp in a single layer — do NOT crowd the pan or they steam instead of sear. Cook 90 seconds per side. Done when they curl into a C shape and turn pink and opaque. DO NOT OVERCOOK — overcooked shrimp are rubbery.

Finish with lemon and zest

Remove pan from heat. Add lemon zest, lime zest, and squeeze lemon juice over shrimp and toss to coat. The lemon sizzles in hot butter and lifts all the fond. Taste one shrimp and adjust seasoning if needed.

Plate and serve

Transfer to wide shallow serving bowl. Scatter chopped parsley over top. Arrange lemon wedges around rim. Place remoulade in small ramekin alongside. Serve at ROOM TEMPERATURE — no chafing dish.

Wave 2 at 2:30 PM

WAVE 1 fires at 1:30 PM using 4 lbs. WAVE 2 fires at 2:30 PM using remaining 4 lbs. Refresh the serving bowl completely — do not pile Wave 2 on top of Wave 1 remnants. Fresh parsley garnish on Wave 2 too.

Kitchen Notes

ORDER 21–25 COUNT SPECIFICALLY. Fire in two batches of 4 lbs each — do not cook all 8 lbs at once.

The cajun butter coating keeps shrimp moist and flavorful at room temp for 90 minutes — no chafing dish needed.

Remoulade must be made the day before — it improves significantly overnight as flavors meld.

Do not season shrimp hours ahead — the salt draws out moisture and results in steamed rather than seared shrimp.

RECIPE 09  ·  SMOKE THURSDAY ALONGSIDE PORK  ·  SLICE THURSDAY NIGHT  ·  SEAR & ASSEMBLE SATURDAY

Smoked Fried Bologna Sandwich

Whole beef bologna chubs smoked Thursday alongside the pork — sliced thick, scored, and seared in cast iron until edges cup and char — assembled in the kitchen with American cheese, onion, and quick pickles — a Louisville lunch-counter legend

3 x 4 LB CHUBS  ·  ~36–45 WHOLE SANDWICHES  ·  ~72–90 HALF SANDWICH PIECES  ·  WAVE 1 AT 1:30 PM  ·  WAVE 2 AT 2:30 PM

Ingredients

  • 3 x 4 lb Boar’s Head Beef Bologna chubs — whole and unsliced — Abbey provides from QFC
  • 7 loaves Texas toast — thick sliced
  • Yellow mustard — classic French’s in squeeze bottle — not dijon, not spicy brown
  • American cheese slices — standard individually wrapped — melted on bologna in pan
  • White onion — 2–3 medium, sliced paper thin
  • Quick pickles — from Recipe 03, well drained
  • Butter — for toasting bread and light pan wipe

Method

THURSDAY — SMOKE THE BOLOGNA

Smoke the chubs whole

Place all 3 bologna chubs whole and unsliced directly on the smoker grate alongside the pork shoulders. Smoke at 225–250°F for 1.5–2 hours. No wrapping needed. Bologna is already fully cooked — you are adding smoke flavor and developing the casing.

Know when it’s done

Pull when the casing is deep mahogany colored and slightly tacky to the touch. The surface should look like it has a thin lacquer. Judge by appearance and time — internal temp is not relevant.

Cool and refrigerate

Remove from smoker and let cool to room temperature. Do NOT slice yet. Wrap whole chubs tightly in plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight. Slicing cold smoked bologna is significantly easier and cleaner than slicing warm.

THURSDAY NIGHT — SLICE & PREP

Slice the bologna

Slice each chub into 1/2 inch thick rounds. Each 4 lb chub yields approximately 12–15 slices. 3 chubs = 36–45 slices total. Layer between parchment paper on a sheet pan, cover, and refrigerate overnight.

Score each slice

Make 4 small cuts around the outer edge of each slice — at 12, 3, 6, and 9 o’clock positions. Cut only 1/2 inch in from the edge, not through the center. This prevents the slice from ballooning during the sear and ensures even contact with the cast iron.

Prep the onion

Slice white onion paper thin. Separate into rings. Store covered in the refrigerator overnight.

SATURDAY — SEAR & ASSEMBLE

Toast the bread

Butter each slice of Texas toast lightly on one side. Toast buttered side down in a hot pan or on a flat top until deep golden brown. Keep warm in a low oven covered with foil. Toast in batches ahead of each wave.

Heat the cast iron

Heat cast iron skillet over MEDIUM to MEDIUM-HIGH heat. A light wipe of butter in the pan is all you need. Bologna will render its own fat as it cooks.

Sear the bologna

Add scored bologna slices in a single layer — do NOT crowd the pan. Work in batches of 4–6 slices. Cook 2–3 minutes first side until deeply browned and starting to char at the edges. The edges should curl slightly upward as the fat contracts.

Flip and melt the cheese

Flip each slice. Immediately lay one slice of American cheese on top. Cook second side 2–3 minutes. The cheese will melt completely over the bologna — it may begin to brown slightly at the edges. That is correct.

Assembly — Standard FireHorse Build

Every sandwich is built the same way in the kitchen. Cheese and onion are standard on every sandwich. Guests remove what they don’t want.

1.Bottom slice of toasted Texas toast — toasted side up 2.Thin stripe of yellow mustard across the toast 3.Fried smoked bologna slice — melted American cheese on top 4.Small pile of well-drained quick pickles 5.2–3 rings of paper-thin white onion 6.Top slice of toasted Texas toast — toasted side down

Plating & Presentation

Cut and plate

Cut each assembled sandwich diagonally in half. Arrange cut side up on a large platter so guests can see the layers — smoked bologna, melted cheese, pickles. The cross-section is the presentation. Garnish the platter edge with a few extra pickle slices.

Wave 2 at 2:30 PM

Repeat the exact same process for the second wave. Refresh the platter completely — do not pile new pieces on top of old ones. Fresh platter, fresh garnish, same build every time.

Kitchen Notes

Smoke the chubs whole and unsliced Thursday alongside the pork. Slicing cold smoked bologna Thursday night is easier, cleaner, and gives a better cut surface.

Score the edges before searing — unscored bologna curls into a bowl and only the edges touch the pan. Scored bologna lies flat with a natural slight cup that holds the cheese perfectly.

Cast iron only — a non-stick pan will not get hot enough to develop the char. The char is the whole point of this sandwich.

Do not crowd the pan — batches of 4–6 slices only. Crowding drops the temperature and steams instead of sears.

American cheese only — not cheddar, not provolone, not Swiss. American melts into liquid and slightly chars at the edges in cast iron. That is the correct result.

Every sandwich gets cheese and onion — standard build in the kitchen. Guests remove what they don’t want. This keeps the kitchen moving fast during a wave.

3 chubs = ~36–45 whole sandwiches = ~72–90 half pieces across two waves. Cut side up on the platter — guests should see the layers. That presentation is what sells the sandwich before anyone takes a bite.
